Adobo Recipe

Adobo is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Filipino cuisine, known for its rich, savory, and tangy flavor. Traditionally made by simmering meat in a mixture of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns, Adobo showcases the Filipino skill of creating deep flavor using simple ingredients. Its balance of saltiness and acidity, combined with tender meat and a fragrant sauce, makes it a comforting meal enjoyed across the Philippines.

ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into serving pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup vinegar
  • 1 head garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium onion (optional, pero adds sweetness)
  • 2–3 pcs bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole peppercorn (or cracked pepper)
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional, pampabalance)
  • 1/2–1 cup water
  • Oil for sautéing
  • Salt (only if needed—it usually doesn’t)

    • Instructions

    1. In a large bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, crushed garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight for more flavor).
    2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. If using onion, sauté until translucent.
    3. Add the marinated chicken pieces (reserve the marinade) and cook until they are lightly browned on all sides.
    4. Pour in the reserved marinade and add water to cover the chicken halfway. Bring to a boil.
    5. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the chicken is tender. Stir occasionally.
    6. If desired, add brown sugar to balance the flavors. Adjust seasoning with salt if necessary.
    7. Once the chicken is cooked and the sauce has thickened to your liking, remove from heat.
    8. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy your delicious Adobo!
